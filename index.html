Hi, my name is Varvara! I’m an independent researcher working at the intersection of machine learning and physics. My research focuses on physics-informed learning and spectral representation methods — lately on physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) and eigenfunction features whose geometry is learned rather than fixed. I’m interested in how physical structure — energies, operators, symmetries — can be built into the way models represent data, and more broadly in the AI4Physics and scientific-ML program.

My recent work, PIEFS: Physics-Informed Eigenfunction Features with Learnable Scaling, was accepted to the ICML 2026 AI4Physics Workshop. It replaces hand-picked spectral feature scaling with a learnable metric trained under a modified Dirichlet energy — joint work with Timur Lidzhiev and Alexander Tarakanov.

I received my BSc in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science (machine-learning track) from HSE University. I’m now looking to continue this line of research through doctoral study, and I’d be glad to connect with groups working on physics-informed and scientific machine learning.

If you’d like to talk about my work — or just chat — feel free to reach me by email. I’ll be happy to answer any questions!
